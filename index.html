How to Enable Linux on a Chromebook (Crostini)

Step 1: Check if your Chromebook supports Linux

  • Go to Settings > About ChromeOS > Additional details
  • Make sure your Chromebook is up-to-date (preferably ChromeOS 89+)
  • Not all devices support Linux. You can check the official supported device list here.

Step 2: Open Settings

  • Click on the clock in the bottom-right corner
  • Click the gear icon to open Settings

Step 3: Enable Linux (Beta)

  • Scroll down to "Developers" in the sidebar
  • Click "Turn On" next to Linux development environment (Beta)
  • Click Next

Step 4: Set up Linux

  • Choose your desired username and disk size
  • Click Install (this can take a few minutes)

Step 5: Launch the Linux Terminal

  • Once installed, a Terminal app will appear in your app launcher
  • You can now install Linux apps using apt (like on Debian)

Optional: Update your Linux environment

sudo apt update && sudo apt upgrade
